# ASCI Compliance Codes & Linguistic Risk Taxonomy

## 1. ASCI Code Quick Reference
The Advertising Standards Council of India (ASCI) enforces four core chapters governing honesty, decency, safety, and fairness:
- **Chapter I**: Truthful & Honest Representation (no misleading claims).
- **Chapter II**: Decency in Advertising (avoiding grave or widespread offense).
- **Chapter III**: Safeguards against Hazardous or Harmful Products/Situations.
- **Chapter IV**: Fairness in Competition.

## 2. Linguistic Risk Categories
When analyzing copy crises, class them into these risk zones:
- **SR-01 (Double Entendre)**: Colloquial phrases containing hidden sexual, political, or offensive connotations when translated or read in regional contexts.
- **SR-02 (Mitigation of Sacred Symbolism)**: Commercialization of mythological characters or sacred terminology causing deep religious friction.
- **SR-03 (Classist/Elitist Sloganeering)**: Slogans that inadvertently mock, demean, or exclude specific socio-economic classes.
- **SR-04 (Regional Translation Hazard)**: Direct translations of global taglines that turn into nonsense or offensive terms in local Indian dialects.
