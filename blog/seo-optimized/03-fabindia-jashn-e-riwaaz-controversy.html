FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz: Where Cultural Celebration Meets Controversy

How an Urdu phrase for a Hindu festival collection sparked a national debate on cultural appropriation, religious identity, and the challenges of inclusive marketing in polarized times.

Published: January 25, 2024 | Reading Time: 9 minutes | BrandCheck Pro - IIMC Thesis Project

The Campaign That Question Cultural Boundaries

In October 2021, as India prepared for the Diwali festival season, FabIndia - one of India's most respected ethnic wear brands - launched its festive collection with a name that would ignite one of the most complex brand crisis examples in India. The collection, titled "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" (Celebration of Traditions in Urdu), became the epicenter of a fierce debate about cultural appropriation, religious identity, and the politics of language in modern India.

Unlike typical marketing campaign failures born from obvious insensitivity, the FabIndia controversy existed in a gray zone where reasonable people disagreed passionately. The incident reveals the challenges brands face when navigating India's increasingly polarized cultural landscape.

Understanding the Jashn-e-Riwaaz Controversy

FabIndia announced its festive collection via Twitter, showcasing traditional Indian wear with the caption promoting "Jashn-e-Riwaaz." Almost immediately, social media users began questioning why a Hindu festival collection carried an Urdu name instead of a Hindi or Sanskrit alternative.

The Arguments Against Jashn-e-Riwaaz

Critics of the campaign raised several concerns that resonated with millions of Indians:

  • Cultural Mismatch: Using an Urdu phrase for a Hindu festival seemed incongruous to those who view Diwali as specifically Hindu
  • Pattern Recognition: Some saw it as part of a broader trend of "secularizing" Hindu festivals by removing Hindu identifiers
  • Commercial Appropriation: Accusations that the brand profited from Hindu traditions while refusing to acknowledge them
  • Political Context: The timing coincided with rising debates about cultural identity and historical narratives

The Defense of Linguistic Diversity

Defenders of FabIndia countered with equally compelling arguments:

  • Urdu's Indian Roots: Urdu developed in India and represents part of the country's linguistic heritage
  • Ganga-Jamuni Culture: The name reflected India's syncretic traditions where Hindu and Muslim cultural elements blended
  • Artistic Freedom: Brands should have creative liberty in naming collections
  • Inclusion: The name was inclusive, not exclusive, celebrating traditions regardless of religious origin

The Escalation: From Social Media to Political Arena

What began as social media discourse quickly escalated when political figures entered the fray. Members of the ruling party and affiliated organizations publicly criticized FabIndia, amplifying the campaign backlash in India exponentially.

The controversy reached such intensity that #BoycottFabIndia began trending nationally, with some users sharing videos of themselves burning FabIndia products. The incident demonstrates how quickly PR disasters can escalate when political actors engage with brand controversies.

FabIndia's Response Strategy

FabIndia's crisis management approach differed from other advertising controversies in its restraint:

The Quiet Withdrawal

Rather than issuing a formal apology or defending their position, FabIndia simply removed the promotional tweet and reverted to marketing the collection as a generic "Diwali Collection." This understated response:

  • Avoided further fueling the controversy
  • Did not alienate either side of the debate
  • Maintained brand dignity without capitulation
  • Allowed the controversy to dissipate naturally

Business Continuity

Despite the social media storm, FabIndia did not withdraw the actual products or suffer significant business impact. The controversy remained largely confined to digital discourse without affecting physical store operations.

Historical Context: Urdu and Indian Identity

To understand the FabIndia controversy, one must appreciate the complex history of Urdu in India:

The Ganga-Jamuni Tradition

Urdu developed in India during the medieval period, incorporating Persian, Arabic, Turkish, and local Indian languages. For centuries, it served as a language of poetry, administration, and culture across religious communities. The phrase "Ganga-Jamuni tehzeeb" refers to the syncretic culture of the Gangetic plains where Hindu and Muslim traditions intertwined.

Partition and Linguistic Politics

The 1947 Partition of India changed Urdu's position dramatically. As Pakistan adopted Urdu as its national language, the language became politically charged in India. While Urdu remains an official language and vibrant literary tradition, it has become increasingly identified with Muslim culture in popular perception.

Contemporary Language Politics

In contemporary India, language has become a marker of identity and political affiliation. The FabIndia controversy must be understood within this context, where linguistic choices carry symbolic weight beyond their literal meaning.

Marketing Lessons from the Jashn-e-Riwaaz Controversy

The FabIndia case offers unique insights for marketers navigating cultural sensitivities:

1. Context Matters More Than Intent

FabIndia's intent - celebrating traditions - was positive. However, the political and social context of 2021 made the execution problematic. Marketers must assess not just what they mean, but how messages will be received in current contexts.

2. Language is Never Neutral

In diverse, politicized markets, linguistic choices carry meaning beyond dictionary definitions. Brands must recognize that words evoke histories, identities, and emotions that cannot be ignored.

3. Silence Can Be Strategic

FabIndia's decision to quietly withdraw without extensive comment proved effective. Sometimes, the best crisis management is avoiding escalation rather than winning the argument.

4. Brand Equity Provides Resilience

FabIndia's decades-long reputation for authentic Indian craftsmanship provided a buffer that newer or less-established brands might not enjoy. Strong brand equity helps weather controversies.

FAQ: FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz Controversy

What was the FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy?

The FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy erupted in October 2021 when the ethnic wear brand launched its Diwali collection under the name "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" - an Urdu phrase meaning "celebration of traditions." Social media users criticized the use of an Urdu name for a Hindu festival, accusing the brand of cultural appropriation and deliberate omission of Hindu terminology. The controversy gained traction when political figures joined the debate, leading FabIndia to remove the promotional tweet.

Why did people object to the name Jashn-e-Riwaaz?

Critics objected to "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" for several reasons: they viewed using an Urdu name for a Hindu festival as inappropriate cultural mixing, some saw it as part of a pattern of Hindu traditions being "secularized" by removing Hindu identifiers, others believed it was commercial appropriation without genuine cultural understanding, and political groups used it to advance narratives about cultural erosion. However, defenders noted that Urdu has deep roots in Indian culture and that linguistic diversity should be celebrated.

How did FabIndia respond to the Jashn-e-Riwaaz backlash?

FabIndia responded by removing the controversial promotional tweet within 24 hours of the backlash beginning. However, unlike some other brands that issued formal apologies, FabIndia chose to simply revert to traditional "Diwali Collection" naming without extensive public comment. This understated approach avoided further fueling the controversy while quietly addressing the concerns raised.

Was the FabIndia controversy justified?

Opinions on whether the controversy was justified remain deeply divided. Critics argue that linguistic diversity is part of India's heritage and that objecting to Urdu reflects growing intolerance. Supporters of the backlash maintain that brands should respect the specific cultural and religious context of festivals they profit from. The case illustrates how cultural sensitivity is subjective and context-dependent in diverse societies.

What can brands learn from the FabIndia controversy?

Key lessons include: recognizing that language carries political weight in diverse markets, understanding that positive intent doesn't guarantee positive reception, considering timing and current social climate in campaign planning, having protocols for quietly addressing concerns without amplifying controversies, and building strong brand equity that provides resilience during crises.

Conclusion: Navigating Cultural Complexity in Indian Marketing

The FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy illustrates the complex terrain modern brands must navigate in India. What might seem like a beautiful, inclusive linguistic choice can become a lightning rod for deeper social tensions. The incident reveals that marketing campaign failures aren't always about obvious errors - sometimes, they're about mismatches between creative vision and cultural context.

For marketers, the case underscores the importance of comprehensive brand safety analysis that goes beyond profanity filters to assess cultural, historical, and political implications. In an era of rapid social media amplification, brands cannot afford to discover controversies after launch.

Ultimately, the FabIndia controversy raises deeper questions about India's evolving identity and the place of linguistic and cultural diversity in commercial spaces. As these debates continue, brands must develop sophisticated capabilities for navigating them - or risk becoming case studies in what not to do.
