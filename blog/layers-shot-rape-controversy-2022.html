Case Study: Layer's Shot Body Spray Controversy (2022)

98%
Backlash Threat Score

In June 2022, the body spray brand Layer'r Shot (manufactured by Adjavis Venture) broadcast two commercials during a high-profile cricket match. Designed with aggressive double-entendres, the scripts triggered an unprecedented level of nationwide public disgust, regulatory intervention, and legal action.

“Trivializing sexual violence or utilizing gang-rape tropes for commercial hook value is the ultimate advertising boundary failure. There is zero tolerance for predatory indecent subtext.”

1. The Script Mechanics and Moral Infraction

The advertisements utilized deliberate semantic setups to imply an impending sexual assault. In one commercial, four men stood behind a woman in a store saying, "Hum char aur ye sirf ek" (We are four and she is just one), before introducing the body spray. The second set up a scenario in a bedroom where a group of men enter a couple's space and make comments suggesting they would sexually assault the woman, only to pivot to spraying the perfume.

This went far beyond poor taste, representing a catastrophic violation under **ASCI Chapter II (Decency & Depiction of Violence/Harm against Women)**. The public, activists, and industry experts immediately pointed out that the commercials normalized predatory behavior and rape culture, triggering massive online campaigns demanding a boycott and severe punishment for the brand.

2. Unprecedented Regulatory and Legal Recourse

The fallout was swift and severe. The Union Ministry of Information and Broadcasting (I&B) took the rare step of directing Twitter and YouTube to immediately pull down the videos, stating they were highly detrimental to the portrayal of women and violated the IT Rules of 2021. Concurrently, the Delhi Commission for Women (DCW) stepped in, filing a formal complaint that led the Delhi Police to register an FIR under Section 67 of the IT Act.

The manufacturing entity, Adjavis Venture, issued a public apology claiming the ads had passed mandatory third-party checks. This case highlights that reliance on checkbox legal compliance is no shield against massive ethical deficits, and creative teams must undergo rigorous, context-aware gender sensitivity reviews.
