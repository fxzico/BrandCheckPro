FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz Controversy: When Language Choice Becomes Political

How an Urdu phrase for Diwali became a flashpoint in India's cultural politics. A case study on linguistic risk in festive marketing and the politicization of language.

In October 2021, just before Diwali, FabIndia—a brand celebrated for promoting Indian textiles and crafts—found itself at the center of a linguistic controversy that revealed how deeply language is entangled with identity politics in contemporary India.

The Campaign: Jashn-e-Riwaaz

FabIndia launched a promotional campaign for their Diwali collection using the Urdu phrase "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" (जश्न-ए-रवायज़), which translates to "Celebration of Traditions." The campaign featured models in traditional Indian attire showcasing festive wear for the Diwali season.

The Creative Intent

FabIndia's choice likely stemmed from:

  • A desire to sound poetic and sophisticated
  • Urdu's historical association with high culture in India
  • The brand's tradition of using diverse cultural expressions
  • Appealing to an urbane, cosmopolitan audience

Why the Backlash?

The Language-Politics Nexus

In contemporary India, language has become increasingly politicized. Urdu, though historically a language of Indian culture and poetry, is now predominantly associated with Muslim identity in popular discourse. Using Urdu to describe a Hindu festival was interpreted by many as:

  • Cultural appropriation or erasure
  • Pandering to specific communities while ignoring Hindu sentiments
  • Promoting "secular" values at the expense of Hindu traditions
  • Commercial exploitation of Hindu festivals without respecting their cultural context

The BJP MP's Intervention

The controversy gained national traction when BJP MP Tejasvi Surya tweeted:

"Deepavali is not Jashn-e-Riwaaz. This deliberate attempt of abrahamisation of Hindu festivals, depicting models without traditional Hindu attires, must be called out."

This political intervention transformed a marketing controversy into a national debate about cultural identity.

FabIndia's Response: Strategic Retreat

Unlike some brands that stand their ground, FabIndia chose to de-escalate quickly:

  • Removed the "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" terminology
  • Rebranded the collection with neutral terminology
  • Did not issue a confrontational statement
  • Maintained focus on the products rather than the controversy

Key Lessons for Brand Managers

1. Language Carries Political Weight

In polarized times, language choices are never neutral. Words carry historical, religious, and political baggage that can activate unexpected responses.

2. Context Determines Appropriateness

While Urdu is beautiful and historically Indian, its contemporary political associations made it inappropriate for a Hindu festival campaign during a period of heightened religious identity politics.

3. Your Audience is Not Monolithic

FabIndia likely tested this campaign with their cosmopolitan target audience and found no issues. However, in the social media age, campaigns are visible to—and judged by—audiences far beyond the intended target.
