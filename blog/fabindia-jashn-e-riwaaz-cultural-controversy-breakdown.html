FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz: When Language Became Political

The FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy stands as a stark reminder that in India's increasingly polarized linguistic landscape, even word choice carries profound political weight. What began as a seemingly innocent festive marketing campaign quickly spiraled into a nationwide debate about cultural identity and linguistic imposition.

What Was the FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz Campaign?

In October 2021, just before the Diwali festive season, FabIndia—a premium Indian ethnic wear brand known for promoting traditional crafts and textiles—launched its festive collection under the banner "Jashn-e-Riwaaz." The campaign featured models in traditional attire with the Urdu phrase prominently displayed.

The Creative Concept Behind the Ad

The campaign was designed to showcase FabIndia's Diwali collection, featuring traditional Indian wear for the festive season. The creative team chose "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" (Celebration of Traditions) as the campaign tagline, apparently intending to evoke the syncretic cultural heritage that FabIndia has long celebrated.

The collection featured sarees, kurtas, and other ethnic wear in festive colors, marketed as perfect attire for Diwali celebrations. The photography and styling emphasized traditional Indian aesthetics with a contemporary twist.

The Campaign's Strategic Intent

From FabIndia's perspective, the campaign aimed to:

  • Position the brand as celebrating India's pluralistic cultural heritage
  • Create an emotional connection through evocative Urdu poetry-style language
  • Differentiate from competitors using more generic festive messaging
  • Appeal to cosmopolitan urban consumers appreciative of linguistic diversity
"The creative team failed to anticipate how linguistic choices have become politically charged in contemporary India. This represents a classic SR-05 (Linguistic Identity / Cultural Ownership) mismatch where seemingly poetic language triggered identity-based resistance."

Why Did the FabIndia Ad Spark Such Intense Backlash?

The FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy cannot be understood without examining the broader political and social context of language politics in India. The backlash emerged from multiple intersecting sensitivities.

The Diwali-Urdu Disconnect

Critics immediately pointed out what they saw as a fundamental misalignment: using an Urdu phrase to market products for Diwali, a Hindu festival. The objection wasn't merely aesthetic—it was rooted in a political narrative that positions Urdu as a 'Muslim language' and Hindi/Sanskrit as 'Hindu languages.'

Opponents argued that if FabIndia was marketing for Diwali, they should use Hindi terms like "Tyohar" (festival) or Sanskrit-derived terminology rather than Persian/Urdu phrases. The use of "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" was framed as deliberate cultural erasure.

The 'Urdu Imposition' Narrative

The controversy tapped into a growing political narrative about "Urdu imposition" in Indian public life. Right-wing commentators and social media influencers framed the campaign as part of a broader pattern where Hindu festivals and traditions were being stripped of their cultural specificity.

Hashtags like #BoycottFabIndia and #FabIndiaAntiHindu began trending within hours, garnering millions of impressions and calls for economic retaliation against the brand.

The Historical Context

To understand the intensity of the backlash, one must recognize that language politics in India has deep historical roots:

  • The Hindi-Urdu controversy dates back to colonial and partition-era politics
  • Urdu became associated with Muslim identity in post-independence India
  • Hindi promotion became linked to Hindu nationalist movements
  • Festivals like Diwali have become symbolic battlegrounds for cultural ownership

FabIndia's campaign inadvertently walked into this minefield without apparent awareness of the political implications.

The Immediate Fallout: Campaign Withdrawal

Within days of the campaign launch, FabIndia faced an escalating crisis:

  • Social media exploded with criticism and boycott calls
  • Political figures and influencers amplified the controversy
  • News channels covered the backlash extensively
  • Competitors watched carefully to avoid similar mistakes

FabIndia's Response

FabIndia responded swiftly to contain the damage:

  • The "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" phrase was removed from all marketing materials within 72 hours
  • The campaign continued with generic festive messaging
  • The company issued a statement emphasizing respect for all traditions
  • No direct apology was issued, but the withdrawal signaled acknowledgment
"FabIndia is a brand that respects all religions and traditions. Our festive collection 'Jashn-e-Riwaaz' was meant to celebrate the traditions and rituals of Indian festivals. We would like to clarify that the phrase simply means 'celebration of traditions.'"
— FabIndia Official Statement

ASCI Guidelines and Regulatory Perspective

While the FabIndia campaign was not banned by ASCI, the controversy highlighted important considerations under ASCI Chapter II regarding respect for cultural and religious sentiments.

Relevant ASCI Code Sections

  • Clause 1: Advertisements shall not offend decency and propriety
  • Clause 2: Advertisements shall not deride any race, caste, color, creed, or nationality
  • Clause 6: Advertisements shall not exploit religious sentiments

The controversy raised questions about whether using certain languages for specific religious festivals could be considered offensive to cultural sensibilities under a conservative interpretation of these guidelines.

Critical Brand Safety Lessons from the FabIndia Crisis

The FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz controversy offers vital lessons for brand managers, copywriters, and creative directors working in India's complex linguistic environment:

1. Language is Political

In contemporary India, language choices carry political weight that transcends simple communication. Words aren't neutral—they're markers of identity, history, and political affiliation. Brand managers must understand the linguistic politics of their target markets.

2. Religious Festivals Are Sensitive Territory

Major religious festivals like Diwali, Eid, and Christmas have become sites of cultural contestation. Marketing campaigns for these occasions face heightened scrutiny and must navigate carefully between celebration and appropriation.

3. Syncretism Can Backfire

While India's syncretic cultural heritage is genuine, invoking it in marketing can trigger backlash from those who view such messaging as undermining distinct religious identities. What feels inclusive to some feels erasing to others.

4. Test with Politically Diverse Groups

Standard focus groups may not capture politically sensitive reactions. Campaigns should be tested with audiences across the political and cultural spectrum to identify potential flashpoints.

5. Monitor Linguistic Politics

The language politics landscape shifts rapidly. Terms that were acceptable yesterday may become controversial today. Brands need monitoring systems to track evolving sensitivities.

Frequently Asked Questions About the FabIndia Controversy

Why was the FabIndia Jashn-e-Riwaaz campaign controversial?
The campaign used an Urdu phrase ('Jashn-e-Riwaaz') to market Diwali products. Critics argued this was inappropriate for a Hindu festival, accusing the brand of cultural appropriation and 'Urdu imposition.' The controversy became politicized with accusations that FabIndia was deliberately erasing Hindu cultural identity by using Persian/Urdu terminology instead of Hindi or Sanskrit terms.
What does 'Jashn-e-Riwaaz' mean?
'Jashn-e-Riwaaz' is an Urdu phrase translating to 'Celebration of Traditions' in English. 'Jashn' means celebration/festival, and 'Riwaaz' means traditions/customs. While Urdu has been part of India's linguistic landscape for centuries, critics argued using it for Diwali—a Hindu festival—was culturally inappropriate and reflected bias against Hindu cultural expression.
Was the FabIndia campaign banned by ASCI?
No, the campaign was not banned by ASCI. However, facing intense social media backlash and boycott calls, FabIndia voluntarily withdrew the campaign within 72 hours and removed the controversial phrase from marketing materials. The company issued a statement clarifying respect for all religions and traditions.
What lessons can brands learn from the FabIndia controversy?
Key lessons: 1) Understand linguistic politics surrounding religious festivals, 2) Recognize that language choices can become polarizing, 3) Test festival campaigns with diverse demographic groups, 4) Consider how language might be interpreted as cultural appropriation, 5) Have crisis management protocols ready for religious festival campaigns, 6) Use brand safety tools to analyze copy for linguistic sensitivities.
How can brands navigate linguistic sensitivities in festive marketing?
Brands can navigate sensitivities by: 1) Using AI-powered tools like BrandCheck Pro to flag controversial language, 2) Consulting linguistic and cultural experts for religious festivals, 3) Understanding current political climate around language and identity, 4) Using inclusive language that doesn't privilege one tradition over another, 5) Testing campaigns with diverse focus groups, 6) Avoiding unnecessary linguistic experimentation during sensitive periods.
