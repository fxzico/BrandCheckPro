Case Study: FabIndia Urdu Phrasing Controversy (2021)

80%
Backlash Threat Score

In October 2021, ahead of the major Indian festive season of Diwali, ethnic retail brand FabIndia launched its holiday apparel line under the campaign name "Jashn-e-Riwaaz". The phrase, translated from Urdu, means "celebration of tradition." The campaign was accompanied by images of models dressed in traditional ethnic wear, aiming to capture the spirit of Indian heritage and cultural synthesis.

“Linguistic choices in cultural campaigns carry implicit political loads; mismatching language with traditional expectations can trigger rapid ideological polarization.”

1. Linguistic Friction in Festive Marketing

Almost immediately, the campaign triggered a highly coordinated backlash on social media platforms. Critics and political figures accused FabIndia of intentionally "de-Hinduizing" the festival of Diwali by substituting traditional Sanskrit-derived terms with Urdu terminology. Opponents argued that the phrase "Jashn-e-Riwaaz" represented a cultural erasure of the festival's traditional origins, and calls to boycott the brand trended nationally on social networks.

This represents a high-severity **SR-02 (Mitigation of Sacred Symbolism / Linguistic Misalignment)**. The creative team failed to anticipate how language selection would be decoded by groups sensitive to perceived cultural secularization in commercial advertising.

2. Lessons for Brand Governance

Under the ASCI code guidelines, campaigns must respect cultural and religious sentiments. While the brand intended to project an inclusive, poetic image, it ran directly into a highly polarized socio-political climate where language choice is heavily monitored. The controversy highlights that copywriters and marketers must evaluate the cultural connotations of linguistics.

Facing intense economic threats and organized online boycotts, FabIndia deleted all promotional tweets containing the phrase and renamed the collection. This incident demonstrates that when designing festive campaigns, linguistic alignment with target audience expectations is crucial to preventing PR backlash.
