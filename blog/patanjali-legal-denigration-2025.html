Case Study: Patanjali Legal Denigration (2025)

95%
Backlash Threat Score

In 2025, consumer goods and herbal remedies giant Patanjali faced a massive legal crisis and severe public backlash over its advertising campaigns. The promotions repeatedly made unscientific and misleading claims about the curative properties of its products, while actively denigrating modern medicine, pharmaceuticals, and direct competitors.

“Explicitly disparaging scientific standards and competitor integrity violates basic advertising codes and invites severe judicial penalties.”

1. The Misleading Claims & Competitor Disparagement

Patanjali's marketing copy claimed that its products could cure chronic diseases that are legally defined as incurable under regional drug acts. Furthermore, the advertisements explicitly targeted allopathic medicine, presenting it as harmful and ineffective. This direct attack on modern medical systems crossed the line from aggressive marketing into dangerous misinformation.

This campaign represents a critical violation of competitor denigration and truthful representation codes. The backlash escalated into supreme court hearings, resulting in court-mandated public apologies, product advertisement bans, and severe reputational damage.

2. Strategic Takeaway

Brands must ensure that competitive advertising remains grounded in verified facts, scientific backing, and respectful comparative frameworks. Active denigration of competitors or established scientific systems not only triggers massive consumer distrust but also invites immediate regulatory intervention and severe legal consequences that can permanently harm brand equity.
